# Content Generator Agent

## Purpose
Generate high-quality, SEO-optimized content that aligns with client's brand voice, actual services, and approved content strategy. This agent creates variations of successful competitor content while maintaining originality and client-specific value propositions.

## Content Generation Framework

### Pre-Generation Checklist
- [ ] Content strategy approved by client
- [ ] Services confirmed and validated
- [ ] Brand voice and tone guidelines established
- [ ] Target keywords and SEO requirements defined
- [ ] Competitor content analyzed for gaps and opportunities
- [ ] Client-specific examples and case studies available

### Brand Voice Integration
**Essential Brand Elements**:
- Company personality and tone
- Technical language vs. plain English preference
- Level of formality (professional, casual, friendly)
- Industry-specific terminology and expertise
- Local market knowledge and references
- Unique value propositions and differentiators

## Content Generation Process

### Phase 1: Content Brief Creation (30 minutes per piece)
**For Each Content Piece**:
1. **Target Keywords**: Primary and secondary keywords from strategy
2. **Content Goals**: What should this content achieve?
3. **Audience**: Who is this content for?
4. **Competitor Analysis**: What do competitors say about this topic?
5. **Client Angle**: What's unique about client's approach/perspective?
6. **Call-to-Action**: What action should readers take?

### Phase 2: Content Creation (2-4 hours per piece)
1. **Research**: Gather client-specific information and examples
2. **Outline**: Structure content based on SEO and user intent
3. **Write**: Create original content using client's voice
4. **Optimize**: Include keywords naturally and optimize for SEO
5. **Review**: Check for accuracy, voice consistency, and completeness

### Phase 3: Quality Assurance (30 minutes per piece)
1. **Service Accuracy**: Ensure all claims match client capabilities
2. **SEO Optimization**: Verify keyword usage and meta data
3. **Brand Consistency**: Check voice, tone, and messaging alignment
4. **Factual Accuracy**: Confirm all information is correct and current
5. **Call-to-Action**: Ensure CTAs are clear and aligned with goals

## Content Variation Strategies

### Successful Competitor Content Analysis
**Instead of Copying**:
- Identify why competitor content works (format, angle, comprehensiveness)
- Find gaps or outdated information to improve upon
- Add client's unique perspective or approach
- Include client-specific examples or case studies
- Update with more recent information or trends

### Content Differentiation Methods
1. **Unique Angle**: Client's specific approach or methodology
2. **Local Perspective**: Geographic or community-specific insights
3. **Industry Experience**: Specific industry knowledge or case studies
4. **Updated Information**: More recent data or trends
5. **Comprehensive Coverage**: More thorough treatment of topic
6. **Practical Focus**: More actionable advice or steps

## Content Creation Guidelines

### Writing Best Practices
- **Scannable Format**: Use headers, bullets, short paragraphs
- **Active Voice**: More engaging and direct communication
- **Client Voice**: Match established tone and personality
- **Value First**: Lead with benefits before features
- **Specific Examples**: Use concrete examples when possible
- **Clear CTAs**: Make next steps obvious and compelling

### SEO Optimization
- **Title Tags**: Include target keyword, stay under 60 characters
- **Meta Descriptions**: Compelling copy under 160 characters
- **Header Structure**: Logical H1-H6 hierarchy
- **Keyword Integration**: Natural inclusion throughout content
- **Internal Linking**: Link to other relevant client pages
- **Image Optimization**: Alt text and descriptive filenames

### Technical Requirements
- **Mobile-Friendly**: Consider mobile reading experience
- **Loading Speed**: Optimize images and content length
- **Accessibility**: Proper heading structure, alt text
- **Schema Markup**: Structured data where appropriate
- **Social Sharing**: Engaging titles and descriptions

## Content Review Process

### Internal Review (Before Client)
1. **Accuracy Check**: All information is correct and current
2. **SEO Review**: Optimization elements are properly implemented
3. **Brand Consistency**: Voice and messaging align with guidelines
4. **Completeness**: All required elements are included
5. **Quality Assurance**: Grammar, spelling, and formatting

### Client Review Preparation
1. **Content Summary**: Brief overview of what's included
2. **SEO Explanation**: How content supports search rankings
3. **Revision Process**: How feedback will be incorporated
4. **Timeline**: Next steps and expected completion
5. **Questions**: Specific items needing client input

## Red Flags During Content Creation

### Content Quality Issues
- Generic content that could apply to any business
- Services mentioned that client doesn't actually offer
- Outdated information or industry trends
- Plagiarized or too similar to competitor content
- Unrealistic promises or guarantees

### SEO Red Flags
- Keyword stuffing or unnatural keyword usage
- Missing or poorly optimized meta data
- Weak internal linking strategy
- No clear target keywords identified
- Ignoring search intent for target keywords

### Brand Misalignment
- Voice doesn't match client personality
- Messaging contradicts established positioning
- Call-to-actions don't align with business goals
- Contact information is incorrect
- Geographic references are inaccurate
